What follows is not a course development plan. It is the architecture of an educational ecosystem — designed to extend the Strickland model further than any single person can carry it.

The Foundation

What we are building

The Strickland model has always believed that environments shape what people become. This educational ecosystem is built on that same conviction — that how we teach is as important as what we teach, and that the experience of learning about this work should reflect the values of the work itself.

What we are building is not a curriculum. It is a culture of learning.

For decades, the Strickland model spread through relationships, site visits, and the force of one extraordinary person's presence. That is not nothing. It built centers across this country and beyond, and changed thousands of lives. But it is not enough for what comes next.

What this educational ecosystem makes possible is a future in which the model no longer depends on any single person to carry it. Where a community leader in a city Bill Strickland has never visited can learn what they need to know, earn the credential that opens doors, and build something that lasts.

The Architecture

Two tiers. One vision.

This ecosystem operates on two levels by design. The first is open to anyone. The second is for those who are called to build. Together they form something the Strickland model has always embodied: a door open to everyone, and a deeper room for those willing to do the work to enter it.

Tier One — Open

The Open Ecosystem

A curious educator, a community organizer, a workforce developer, a philanthropist — anyone can enter, explore, and take something useful away. The ideas here are portable. A principal can apply Strickland thinking to a school. A manager can apply it to a workplace. A neighbor can apply it to a block. This tier asks nothing except curiosity and good faith.

Tier Two — Gated

The Credentialed Pathway

For those called to build. Structured, sequential, and credentialed. It asks more because the work asks more. Those who move through it emerge not just informed but prepared — ready to make the case to a community, a funder, a board, that they understand what it takes to create a center that lasts.

Recognition and Credentials

Three levels. Each one earned.

The credentialing system is designed to be legible, stackable, and meaningful at every level. All three designations carry digital badges built for LinkedIn profiles, email signatures, organizational bios, and grant applications — because visibility matters.

Strickland Global Fellow

Level One

Earned through intentional participation in the Strickland Global network, including the annual convening. Attendance alone does not confer this designation. A Fellow has engaged with the community of practice, contributed to shared learning, and made a documented commitment to this work. The badge is annual and dated — renewing each year through continued engagement. Multiple consecutive Fellow badges communicate sustained commitment to any external audience.

Badge: Strickland Global Fellow — 2026

Strickland Global Ambassador

Level Two

Earned through completion of foundational coursework covering the Strickland philosophy, environment, community, and human dignity. This designation signals that a person has internalized the philosophy at a level that allows them to apply it meaningfully in their own context. They may not be building a center — but they are building something better because of what they have learned.

Badge: Strickland Global Ambassador

Strickland Global Certified

Level Three

The designation for those who have completed the full credentialing pathway and demonstrated readiness to develop, lead, or govern a center grounded in the Strickland model. This credential carries a specialization that reflects the specific work the individual is prepared to do. Current specializations include Founder, Board Governance, and Executive Leadership. Additional specializations will be developed as the network grows.

Badge: Strickland Global Certified: Founder

September 2026 Launch

The Founders Course

The first credentialed offering in the Strickland Global educational ecosystem and the gateway to the Strickland Global Certified: Founder designation. A five to six week course designed for individuals and teams who are exploring or actively pursuing the creation of a center grounded in the Strickland model.

01

The Strickland Philosophy

Establishes the intellectual and moral foundation of the model. Learners engage deeply with the core principles — that environments shape behavior, that people are assets, that beauty and dignity are not luxuries but prerequisites. This unit does not simply describe the philosophy. It asks learners to examine their own assumptions about community, potential, and what institutions owe the people they serve.

02

Community Need and Engagement

Moves from philosophy into context. Before a center can be built, a community must be understood. This unit develops the capacity to assess genuine community need, build authentic relationships with local stakeholders, and design programs that respond to what employers and community members actually require — rather than what founders assume they need.

03

Funding and Financial Sustainability

Addresses the practical architecture of keeping a center alive. Learners engage with funding models, philanthropic relationships, workforce development funding streams, and the financial structures that distinguish centers that thrive from those that struggle. This unit draws directly from the experience of existing centers in the Strickland network.

04

Long Range Planning

Prepares founders to think beyond launch. A center that opens is not a center that lasts. This unit addresses governance, strategic planning, community integration, organizational culture, and the leadership practices that sustain a center through its inevitable challenges and transitions.
